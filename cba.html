EV Tax Credit Cost-Benefit Analysis

"Should the One Big Beautiful Bill Act have ended the $7,500 EV Tax Credit?"

I'll be using resources from the Congressional Budget Office and Joint Committee on Taxation to answer this question. The (now expired, as of Sept. 30) federal EV tax credit incentivized consumers by compensating them up to $7,500 when they bought a new electric vehicle. The hope was that a credit on electric vehicles would shrink the price disparity between EVs and combustion vehicles—or even make the EV cheaper.

Naturally, this credit had a large price tag. It was estimated to cost the U.S. government ~14 - 20 billion dollars between 2022 and 2032, which was the intended timeframe for this project before OBBA ended it.
